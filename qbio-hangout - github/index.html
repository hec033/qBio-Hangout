We believe that the heart of problem solving and discovering novel solutions lie in critical thinking - unrestricted by fancy equipment and tedious laboratory procedures....

Here's how it works!

1) Initiate

We announce experiment details prior to experimentation. This information includes strain names, induction details, and experimental start times. Due to the wide range of experiments that can be performed, additional factors may be announced specific to individual experiments.

2) Participate

You model behavior and response of each strain to specified inductions and develop time series predictions for each gene. You submit these time series predictions in the form of a CSV file prior to a specified deadline.

3) Compare

We begin experimentation in the lab and provide real time comparisons of your predictions with the actual results. From these results, models can be modified and updated to provide more accurate predictions. The person who most accurately modeled each gene is declared the winner for this experiment competition.

4) Collaborate

At the conclusion of the experiment, the winner provides input on the next experimental round. We will coordinate efforts to set up the experiment dictated by the winner.

Our Mission

qBio Hangout is an online platform seeking to motivate communication between the computational and experimental branches of systems and quantitative biology. The competition is a means to evaluate and further develop systems models through friendly rivalry among competitors. It also reaches out to individual researchers and allows their ideas to come to leave the realm of theory and manifest themselves inside reality. Ultimately, our goal is to experimentally evaluate existing models and explore novel ideas to advance the fields of systems and quantitative biology.

Our Technology and Strains

Our Technology

The lab has developed its unique own state-of-art "small-box" and microfluidic chips for the experiments to run in. The small box will take a image every 10 minutes of the experiments in the microfluidic chip, and then piece them up into time lapses for data analysis and viewing.
